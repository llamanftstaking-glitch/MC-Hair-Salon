# MC Hair Salon & Spa — Product Context

## Product Purpose
Booking and brand website for MC Hair Salon & Spa, an upscale hair salon on the Upper East Side of Manhattan. The site handles appointment booking, service discovery, client account management, staff scheduling, and admin operations. Design serves the business: it must convert UES clientele into bookings.

## Register
product

## Users

**Primary: UES Women (35–65)**
- Upper East Side residents and workers
- Value discretion, polish, and quality over trend
- Book on mobile during commute or desktop at home
- Expect luxury-brand UX — not startup SaaS
- Want fast booking, not friction

**Secondary: New clients discovering via search/Instagram**
- Arriving from @mchairsalon Instagram or Google
- Need social proof fast (gallery, testimonials, pricing transparency)
- First impression = brand impression

**Internal: Staff and Admin**
- Stylists check their daily/weekly schedule
- Owner manages bookings, inventory, analytics via admin panel

**Tone:** Elevated, warm, confident. Not cold luxury. Not girlboss hype. Quietly exceptional — the salon that doesn't need to shout. Copy is concise, direct, never breathless.

**Aesthetic:** Black and gold. Timeless, not trendy. Playfair Display for headings signals heritage. Inter for body signals precision. The combination = "we've been here 15 years and we know what we're doing."

## Anti-References
- No SaaS blue / teal / purple gradient aesthetics
- No startup-y micro-copy ("Let's get started!", "You're all set!")
- No heavy glassmorphism or frosted card grids
- No bubbly rounded cards with icon + heading + text
- No bright white sterile medical spa look
- No Instagram-girl pink or millennial blush

## Strategic Principles
1. **Booking is the primary CTA everywhere.** Every page should funnel toward /book.
2. **Trust signals above the fold.** Gallery, testimonials, years in business — UES clients vet before booking.
3. **Account creation is frictionless.** Google sign-in primary, reducing friction for returning clients.
4. **Mobile-first layout.** Clients book on iPhones. Desktop is secondary.
5. **Admin is internal tooling.** Admin/staff UIs prioritize information density over aesthetics.
